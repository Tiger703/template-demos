The Cellar

Biodynamic Structure

Our wine list rejects the overly manipulated. We seek out growers who farm with intention, producing wines with tension, minerality, and a true sense of place. These are the structural pillars that support the richer courses of the menu.

The Ferment

Unpasteurized Purity

Japanese sake is not merely an alternative to wine; it is a different lens through which to view the food. We serve rare, unpasteurized (namazake) micro-brews that offer lifted aromatics and a clean, umami-rich finish that wine simply cannot achieve.

The Synthesis

The Dual Pairing

Guests are encouraged to experience the "Dual Arc"—alternating between a French wine and a Japanese sake throughout the meal. The contrast between the two liquid philosophies creates a third, entirely new flavor profile on the palate.

Chapter 05 — Atmosphere

Engineering the unseen.

Great food can be ruined by bad lighting or loud acoustics. We obsess over the invisible elements of hospitality to ensure total immersion.

85
Dimness

Acoustic Dampening

Hidden micro-perforated panels absorb ambient echo, ensuring conversations remain private and the kitchen sounds are reduced to a gentle, rhythmic hum.

92
Focus

Pinpoint Lighting

Overhead beams are calibrated to 2700K and angled precisely to illuminate the plate without casting shadows from the guest's hands, leaving the surrounding room in soft darkness.

78
Balance

Thermal Zoning

The counter surface is gently heated, while the ambient air remains crisp. This thermal contrast ensures hot food stays hot longer and the guest remains comfortably alert.
